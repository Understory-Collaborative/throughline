# Brand direction

**Status: Agreed direction.** Captured with the team. The exact tokens that
follow from it live in `tokens.md` and are approved separately.

## The belief

Someone leaving incarceration is a person starting a new life and deserves to be
treated like one. Dignity is essential.

This belief sets three rules the design has to keep.

- **Assume competence, not failure.** Speak to a capable adult. Do not explain
  as if the person caused the problem or cannot follow along.
- **Celebrate progress.** Lead with what the person has done and what they have.
  Do not open every screen with what is missing.
- **No surveillance, no judgment.** Never make someone feel watched, graded, or
  stupid for not knowing how an opaque system works. The system is the hard
  part, not the person.

## How it feels

The feeling, in full. Sitting on a porch on a cool, breezy summer day. A cool,
crisp lemonade in your hand. Leon Bridges playing. A warm breeze kicks up, and
it is welcoming. You know it is going to be ok. You are headed somewhere, and
you are not being rushed. You know your path, and you are getting there in your
own time. A cool rain is coming, and it is a welcome rain, not a storm. Lilacs
are on the wind. A bullfrog is singing in the creek. The wind moves the leaves
in the trees.

The shorthand:

- **Calm and steady.** Quiet and unhurried. Nothing shouts.
- **Warm and human.** Like a person beside you, not a form or an agency.
- **Hopeful and forward.** You are headed somewhere, in your own time.
- **Welcoming, even the hard parts.** The rain is welcome, not a storm. Warnings
  stay gentle and renewing.

## The look, in words

These directions guide the tokens. They are the why behind the numbers.

- **Color: sun-faded and warm, with a vintage glow.** Warm cream like a porch in
  late light, over deeper mahogany and leather tones. Sage and creek greens for
  the path and the main actions. Honey lemonade for warm highlights. Soft lilac
  on the wind and a cool welcome-rain blue as gentle secondary notes. Colors
  look sun-softened, not printed.
- **Texture: worn-in and familiar.** Like a favorite baseball t-shirt, mahogany,
  and leather. Comfortable and broken-in, never crisp or new.
- **Light: golden hour.** A soft warm glow, like late-afternoon sun, and a faint
  film warmth. Light enough to stay fast on a cheap phone.
- **Type: humanist sans, system fallback.** A warm, highly readable face for a
  person reading under stress, with the device's own font as a fast fallback for
  people on limited data. A warm display face is on the table for headings.
- **Shape: soft and airy.** Rounded corners and generous space. Gentle, never
  crowded or hard.
- **Motion: a breeze.** Slow, soft transitions. Nothing snappy or urgent. Always
  honor reduced motion.

## What this rules out

- **The DMV.** Never the DMV. Nowhere should feel like the DMV. Cold, rushed,
  processed, and made to feel small. That is the enemy.
- Cold, institutional, government-form looks.
- Loud alerts and a wall of red flags for what is missing.
- Anything crisp, clinical, or brand-new feeling.
- Anything that reads as a pity pamphlet or a surveillance dashboard.

## How it shows up in the product

- Progress is visible and affirming. Done steps are celebrated, not just
  checked.
- Gaps are framed as the next step, with where to go, in a calm tone.
- Plain language at a 3rd to 4th grade level. See `voice-and-tone.md`.

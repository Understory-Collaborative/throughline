# Brand direction

**Status: Agreed direction.** Captured with the team. The exact tokens that
follow from it live in `tokens.md` and are approved separately.

## The belief

Someone leaving incarceration is a person starting a new life and deserves to be
treated like one. Dignity is essential.

This belief sets three rules the design has to keep.

- **Assume competence, not failure.** Speak to a capable adult. Do not explain
  as if the person caused the problem or cannot follow along.
- **Celebrate progress.** Lead with what the person has done and what they have.
  Do not open every screen with what is missing.
- **No surveillance, no judgment.** Never make someone feel watched, graded, or
  stupid for not knowing how an opaque system works. The system is the hard
  part, not the person.

## How it feels

- **Calm and steady.** Quiet and unhurried. Nothing shouts.
- **Warm and human.** Like a person beside you, not a form or an agency.
- **Hopeful and forward.** A sense of momentum and a path opening up.

## The look, in words

These directions guide the tokens. They are the why behind the numbers.

- **Color: earthy and warm, with growth greens.** Warm, human neutrals for most
  of the screen. Greens that signal a path and new growth carry the forward
  feeling and the main actions.
- **Type: humanist sans, system fallback.** A warm, highly readable face for a
  person reading under stress, with the device's own font as a fast fallback for
  people on limited data.
- **Shape: soft and airy.** Rounded corners and generous space. Gentle, never
  crowded or hard.

## What this rules out

- Cold, institutional, government-form looks.
- Loud alerts and a wall of red flags for what is missing.
- Anything that reads as a pity pamphlet or a surveillance dashboard.

## How it shows up in the product

- Progress is visible and affirming. Done steps are celebrated, not just
  checked.
- Gaps are framed as the next step, with where to go, in a calm tone.
- Plain language at a 3rd to 4th grade level. See `voice-and-tone.md`.

# Voice and tone

How Throughline sounds. The voice stays steady across the product. The tone
shifts to fit the moment.

## Voice

- **Warm.** We are walking beside the person, not processing them.
- **Plain.** 3rd to 4th grade reading level. Short words. Short sentences.
- **Honest.** We say what the tool can and cannot do, and we point to people for
  the rest.
- **Respectful.** We speak to adults who are owed respect. No shame, no talking
  down.

## Tone by moment

- **Welcome and start.** Steady and encouraging. Lower the stakes.
- **Questions.** Calm and direct. Ask one thing at a time.
- **Results and next steps.** Clear and practical. Tell the person exactly what
  to do next.
- **Gaps and bad news.** Honest and kind. Name the gap, then show the next move
  and where to get help.

## Writing rules in practice

These match the house rules in the root `CLAUDE.md`.

- No em dashes. Use a period or a comma, or rewrite.
- No "it is not this, but that". State the thing directly.
- No clause-colon-clause sentences. Use two sentences.
- Avoid filler and hype. Skip "in conclusion", "the bottom line is", and "moat".
- Run the `/humanizer` skill on drafts.

## Quick examples

Instead of "Your application cannot be processed without proof of residency",
write "You will need one paper that shows where you live. Here is what counts."

Instead of "Utilize the documentation enumerated below", write "Bring the papers
on this list."

## A reading-level check

Before copy ships, read it out loud. If a sentence runs out of breath, cut it.
If a word would stop Marcus (see `../personas/marcus.md`), swap it for a simpler
one.

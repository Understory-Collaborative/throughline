# Personas

Who we build for. Personas keep the work grounded in real lives instead of an
abstract "user".

## How we use them

- Start from a real situation, then check it with people who have lived it.
- Mark anything not yet reviewed with the community as **Draft, needs community
  review**.
- When a design or copy choice is hard, ask what it does for the person in the
  persona.

## The three problem layers

Each persona names three layers of the problem, so we do not solve only the
surface one:

- **External.** The concrete barrier in the world, such as a document
  requirement.
- **Internal.** What the person carries, such as shame, fear, or stress.
- **Philosophical.** The deeper wrong the situation points to, such as a system
  that makes ordinary life hard to re-enter.

First Step Out can move the external layer and ease the internal one. It cannot
fix the philosophical layer alone. Naming that layer keeps us honest about scope
and points to where people and policy carry the work.

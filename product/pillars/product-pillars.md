# Product pillars

These principles decide what we build and how we build it. When a choice is
unclear, return here. If a feature idea works against a pillar, that is a strong
signal to stop and rethink.

## 1. Dignity first

We speak to people as adults who are owed respect. Plain language at a 3rd to
4th grade reading level so it reaches everyone, written for someone reading
under stress. No government compliance tone. No shame, no jargon, no hoops that
exist only to satisfy a system.

## 2. Social justice is the point

This exists to help people leaving incarceration rebuild on fair footing. Every
decision gets weighed against whether it serves that goal or quietly works
against it.

## 3. Built with, not just for

We collaborate with formerly incarcerated individuals through design and
building. Their review carries weight. A persona, a flow, or a sentence is not
finished until the people it serves have shaped it.

## 4. The tool supports the work, it does not replace it

We are building a tool, not a community. When a person, a caseworker, or an
organization should be the one to help, we point there. We do not pretend
software can carry what only people can carry.

## 5. Privacy as safety

Our audience has good reason to be careful with personal information. We collect
the least we can. The first build keeps user data on the device, asks for no
account, and saves no name. Any future change that collects more must clear a
high bar and be explained plainly to the person before it happens.

## 6. Works where people actually are

A phone, often a cheap one. Limited data. Patchy connection. A library computer
on a timer. We build mobile first, keep pages light, and make sure the core
help works under poor conditions. We meet WCAG 2.2 AA so the product works for
people using screen readers, large text, keyboard only, and low vision. See
`design-system/accessibility.md`.

## 7. Trustworthy and current

The steps we give have to be right. State rules change. We treat the question
flow and checklist content as something that must be kept accurate over time,
with a clear owner and a clear way to correct it.

## 8. Ship small, learn, repeat

The first feature is First Step Out, getting your ID back. We do that one thing
well before we widen scope. Real feedback from real use guides what comes next.
